Accra, Ghana

This where I lived with my parents when we moved to Ghana. Accra is the capital of Ghana with about 2 million residents. Ghana is a small country about the size of Minnesota. It is located in West Africa on the Atlantic Coast. It experiences lots of sunshine but not as humid as Missouri. Ghana is the place to be to experience real hospitality and friendly people. While there you must certainly try out spicy delights like 'kelewele' and 'Waakye'!

Kumasi, Ghana

Kumasi is where I went to high school and college. Opoku Ware School was my High School and Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology is where I went to university. I did general science at highschool and proceeded to do Civil Engineering at the University. Kumasi is the 2nd largest city in Ghana with great cultural heritage. It is the home of the Ashanti Kingdom where most of the gold from Ghana comes from. Ghana was actually know as the 'Gold Coast' in the 19th Centuary.

St Louis, United States

St Louis is where I currently live. I have been here for a year now and it has been great!. It is an awesome combination of a city with all desired small town flavors. Little traffic, lower pace of life, friendly people; all in an urban space of littered wih diverse restaurants, lovely parks and spectacular public events! I do miss home but St Louis does well to help me not get too home sick.
